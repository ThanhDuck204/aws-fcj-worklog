## Weekly Notes

This week focused more on completion than adding many new features. After the code review, the team made a few small adjustments so the code would be easier to read and maintain.

Completing Module 6 also helped me review the database choice for the project more clearly. DynamoDB still fits the current MVP direction, while RDS, Aurora, Redshift, and ElastiCache gave me a better view of when other services should be considered.

Over the weekend, I continued working on the project — receiving feedback from the team, finding bugs, and improving features. This was a good chance to gradually refine the product based on real use.

On Sunday, I went to the company to participate in Event 3. It was a valuable opportunity to connect with colleagues and gain new practical insights from the sessions.


---

## Challenges Encountered

* When integrating architecture changes, code, documentation, and mock data needed to stay aligned.
* Code review revealed some parts of the code organization that were not easy enough to read, so they needed adjustment for easier team maintenance.
* Performance optimization needed to focus on areas that actually affect user experience, instead of optimizing parts that are not important yet.
* After completing Module 6, it was important to clearly distinguish DynamoDB, RDS, Aurora, Redshift, and ElastiCache use cases instead of choosing services by feeling.
* Receiving feedback on the project meant continuously finding and fixing bugs, requiring careful tracking of what had been changed.

---

## Lessons Learned

* Code review helps catch small issues and align team coding style.
* Performance optimization should start from the most-used parts instead of being scattered.
* Recording design decisions helps the team understand why the current implementation direction was chosen.
* Module 6 is useful for comparing DynamoDB, RDS, and other AWS data services.
* Continuously finding and fixing bugs based on team feedback is a good way to improve product quality step by step.
* Company events are valuable opportunities to learn practical experience and expand professional networks.
