# Summary Report: AWS Study Group – Community Sharing Session

### Event Information

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Event Name** | AWS Study Group – Community Sharing Session |
| **Date & Time** | May 9th, 2026, 09:00 - 12:00 |
| **Location** | 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City |
| **Role** | Attendee |

---

### Event Objectives

The event focused on several key objectives:

- **Learning Habit Sharing**: Inspire sustainable learning habits within the community
- **AI Tooling Introduction**: Share knowledge on AI applications and Prompt Engineering in software development
- **Career Guidance**: Provide realistic perspectives on the job market and personal growth strategies
- **Modern Development Practices**: Introduce professional software development methodologies and workflows

---

### Speakers

The event featured 4 speakers in the following order:

#### 1. Huynh Hoang Long *(Admin, AWS Study Group)*

**Topic**: *"How to Get Addicted to Learning Like Social Media"*

- Shared strategies for building sustainable and engaging learning habits
- Presented techniques for maintaining long-term motivation in the tech field
- Guided the audience on effective learning methods inspired by behavioral psychology

---

#### 2. Nguyen Tuan Thinh *(Admin, AWS Study Group)*

**Topic**: *"Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality"*

- Introduced **Proptimizer Extension** – a self-built browser extension for automated prompt optimization
- Explored techniques to improve the quality of AI (LLM) generated content
- Live demo of how the extension supports developers when working with AI tools

---

#### 3. Mr. Khang *(Admin, AWS Study Group)*

**Topic**: *"Why You Haven't Started Working Yet"*

- Analyzed the current **IT job market** landscape in Vietnam
- Shared insights on **workplace environments** and company expectations for fresh graduates
- Guided the audience on **self-improvement**: technical skills, soft skills, and professional mindset
- Explained the gap between academic knowledge and real-world job readiness

---

#### 4. Ms. Thao *(Software Developer)*

**Topic**: *"BMAD"* – Build, Measure, Analyze, Deploy

- Introduced the modern software development methodology following the BMAD cycle
- Shared the importance of measurement and analysis during the product development process
- Presented Continuous Deployment practices in real-world environments

---

### Key Takeaways

#### 1. Learning Habits & Motivation
- Learned strategies to make studying as engaging as social media
- Understood the importance of consistent, daily learning habits
- Explored techniques for maintaining long-term motivation in technical learning

#### 2. AI-Assisted Development
- Gained insight into how AI tools support the entire software development lifecycle
- Learned about **Automated Prompt Engineering** and real-world applications
- Discovered **Proptimizer Extension** – a community-built tool for prompt optimization

#### 3. Career Development
- Received practical guidance on professional job readiness preparation
- Understood the gap between academic training and real workplace expectations
- Learned about industry expectations, requirements, and workplace culture in IT

#### 4. Modern Development Practices
- Explored the **BMAD** methodology: Build → Measure → Analyze → Deploy
- Understood the importance of measurement and analysis in product development
- Learned about Continuous Deployment practices in real-world environments

### Personal Reflection

This event was incredibly valuable for my internship journey. The speakers provided practical insights directly applicable to my current learning path with AWS.

**Thinh's** session on the Proptimizer Extension was particularly impressive – seeing a community member build their own tool to solve real problems, not just study theory, was truly inspiring.

**Khang's** talk on the job market gave me a clearer picture of the gap between my current knowledge and what companies actually need – a strong motivation to proactively upgrade my skills every day.

Most importantly, this event helped me realize that the AWS Study Group community is not just a place to learn technical skills, but also a place for career guidance and connecting with like-minded individuals.
